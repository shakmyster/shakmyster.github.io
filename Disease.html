    What is Nec fasc?Necrotizing fasciitis is a rapidly progressive infection of the fascial planes with extensive secondary damage and necrosis of the surrounding soft tissues.     Epididymitis is a medical condition caused by a bacterial infection. Escherichia coli (E. coli) is the most common offending agent in children or elderly patients. In sexually active men, Chlamydia trachomatis and Neisseria gonorrhoeae should be considered. Epididymitis is characterized by gradual onset of pain, fever, and chills with warmth and swelling of the scrotum. The testicle can also be tender and swollen. Physical examination reveals a retained cremasteric reflex. Doppler ultrasound will show patent blood flow. Urine culture and/or gram stain and culture of fluid or swab from the urethra is confirmatory. Antibiotics are used to treat epididymitis. The treatment of choice is often azithromycin and cefixime to cover both Chlamydia trachomatis and Neisseria gonorrhoeae. Doxycycline may be used as an alternative if an allergy to azithromycin is noted. Cephalexin can be used if E. coli is the culprit. Hospitalization is indicated for severe cases. Surgical removal of the epididymitis is rarely indicated.

    Pleural effusion is an abnormal collection of fluid in the pleural space of the lung. There are four types of fluid that can accumulate in the pleural space, including serous fluid (hydrothorax), blood (hemothorax), chyle (chylothorax) and pus (empyema). Patients most commonly experience dyspnea, cough and chest pain. On exam, pleural effusions will present as dullness to percussion, decreased tactile fremitus, a pleural friction rub, diminished breath sounds and asymmetrical chest expansion. If the patient has night sweats, fever, hemoptysis and weight loss, tuberculosis should be suspected. If the patient has increasing lower extremity edema, orthopnea, distended neck veins and paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea, congestive heart failure should be considered. Diagnosis of pleural effusion can be made with a chest xray, which will show radiopaque region with blunted costophrenic angles (300 ml of fluid must be present to have costophrenic blunting). If the pleural cavity contains both air and fluid, then the fluid will form a fluid level on xray.
